Panel Test

Each caution/warning panel includes a TEST function that simulates the pre-start lamp test.

Tapping the TEST control toggles the displayed lamps on and off, showing which lights would normally be illuminated during a pre-start check.

Bell 214ST

On the C/W panel, tap the TEST PNL area in the center of the panel. This toggles the test illumination on/off.

Bell 214B

On the C/W panel, tap the round TEST push button at the lower right of the panel.

Tip: Use the panel test to familiarise yourself with the location of each light before you need them in an emergency.

Light Colours Explained

The caution panel uses standard aviation colour coding to convey urgency:

RED — Warning (immediate action required) YELLOW — Caution (abnormal, monitor) AMBER — Caution (advisory, monitor) GREEN — Advisory / Status (system active) RED — Master Caution (press to reset)
ColourMeaningAction
RED Warning — Immediate danger or system failure Immediate action required. Refer to emergency procedure.
YELLOW Caution — Abnormal condition Monitor or take corrective action. Refer to abnormal procedure.
AMBER Caution — Advisory Monitor. May indicate system status change.
GREEN Advisory / Status System is active or in a normal state. No action required.
MASTER Master Caution Press to reset after identifying the triggering system.

Aircraft Types

The app supports two Bell 214 variants:

AircraftPanel LayoutProcedures
Bell 214ST Full C/W panel with FIRE, ENG OUT, MASTER CAUTION, and sub-panel Complete emergency and abnormal procedures
Bell 214B 4-column × 10-row grid with overhead warning lights Procedures from BHT-214B1-FM-1 Rev.8

Select your aircraft type from the landing page. The panel will update to show the correct layout and lights for that type.

The 214ST panel includes engine sub-panel lights (FUEL PRESS, ANTI-ICE, ECU LOCKOUT, OIL, BLOWER OFF, FUEL VALVE) and the chip indicator panel.

Viewing Procedures

Tap any light on the panel to view its associated procedure. Each procedure includes:

  • Procedure type — EMERGENCY or ABNORMAL
  • Title — The system or failure name
  • Indications — What to look for
  • Corrective Action — Step-by-step checklist items
  • Notes — Additional considerations or warnings

Use the BACK button on each procedure page to return to the panel. Use MAIN PAGE to return to the main app.

Tip: The Master Caution light resets after identifying the triggering system, but the individual caution light remains illuminated until the fault is cleared.

Installation

The app is a Progressive Web App (PWA). It must be installed to work offline — a browser bookmark alone will not cache the files. It WILL fail to load if offline.

iOS

  • Open the link in Safari (not Chrome or Firefox).
  • Tap Share → Add to Home Screen. Ensure the Web App toggle is on.
  • Close Safari and launch from your home screen to complete the download.

Android

  • Open the link in Chrome.
  • Tap the menu and choose Add to Home Screen → Install (not "shortcut").
  • Close Chrome and launch from your home screen to complete the download.

Poor connection at startup? If the app stalls on launch while on a weak signal, enable Airplane Mode to force it to load from the offline cache. Once loaded, disable Airplane Mode.

Aeronet Calculator & Fuel Converter

The Aeronet calculator is a tool for tracking daily fuel usage and receipts, designed for operators who need to log fuel uplift and consumption.

Access it from the ⚙ SETUP page under Tools → Aeronet.

Start & End of Day Fuel

  • Set the Start of Day fuel quantity using the slider — the fuel gauge reading at the start of your shift.
  • Set the End of Day fuel quantity using the slider — the fuel gauge reading at the end of your shift.
  • Both values are displayed in LBS and LITERS simultaneously.
  • Values are saved automatically between sessions.

Fuel Receipts

  • Enter up to 5 fuel receipt quantities in LITERS — the unit used by most refueling trucks.
  • The Total Fuel Added field sums all receipt entries automatically.
  • Tap CLEAR to reset all receipt fields to zero (Start/End Fuel sliders are not affected).

Use case: An operator can track fuel added throughout the day (receipts) against the physical fuel gauge reading at start and end of shift to verify fuel usage and identify discrepancies.

Refuel Calculator

The Refuel Calculator helps determine how much fuel to add to reach a desired fuel load.

Access it from the ⚙ SETUP page under Tools → Refuel Calc.

How It Works

  • DESIRED FUEL — Set the target fuel quantity you want on board after refueling.
  • ACTUAL FUEL — Set the current fuel quantity in the tank (from the aircraft gauge).
  • The calculator shows the FUEL REQUIRED in both LBS and LITERS.
  • If the desired fuel is less than actual fuel, the display shows DEFUEL with the amount to remove.
  • Values are saved automatically between sessions.

The calculator uses Jet-A @ 20°C, 0.800 kg/L density for the LBS to Liters conversion. For certified weight and balance calculations, always use the density on the actual fuel slip/BOL.

LBS to Liters Converter

A standalone converter is included below the Refuel Calculator for quick unit conversion.

  • Slide the range control to set the quantity in LBS.
  • The converter instantly displays the equivalent in LITERS.
  • Useful for converting fuel slip quantities or aircraft gauge readings.

Quick reference: 1 US gallon of Jet-A weighs approximately 6.8 lbs. 1 liter weighs approximately 0.8 kg (1.76 lbs).

Note: Jet-A density varies with temperature. These conversions assume 20°C (68°F). For exact fuel calculations, use the density stated on your fuel delivery slip.

MARK Buttons — Quick POI Marking

The flight page includes three MARK buttons positioned around the Ground Speed gauge. These are designed for quick marking of Points of Interest (POIs) during flight — water sources, fire locations, ground equipment, or any other location you need to reference.

The Three MARK Buttons

🔵

Blue MARK

● Blue Pointer

  • Water sources
  • Dip sites
  • Rivers / lakes
  • Refuel points
🟠

Amber MARK

● Amber Pointer

  • Fire locations
  • Hotspots / smoke
  • Burning areas
  • Drop zones
🔴

Red MARK

● Red Pointer

  • Dozer locations
  • Ground crew
  • Landing zones
  • Base / Heli-base

How It Works

  • Single tap — marks the current GPS position as a waypoint and activates its pointer.
  • Double tap — clears that waypoint and hides its pointer.
  • Each MARK button has a distinct colour:
    • Blue — Water sources, dip sites
    • Amber — Fires, hotspots
    • Red — Dozers, ground crew, LZs
  • All three can be used simultaneously to track different types of POIs on the same sortie.

How the Arrows Work

When you mark a waypoint, a relative-bearing pointer appears around the Ground Speed gauge. Here's what it shows you:

Relative-bearing pointers around the gauge — bearing is relative to your heading, distance in nautical miles

Pointer Behaviour

  • The arrow rotates around the gauge to show bearing relative to your current heading.
  • 12 o'clock = directly ahead. 3 o'clock = 90° right. 9 o'clock = 90° left.
  • The distance to the marked point is shown in nautical miles next to each arrow.
  • If a pointer would go off-screen, it clamps to the edge of the screen so you always know which direction to look.
  • Each pointer has its own colour matching its MARK button.

Practical Use Cases

ScenarioHow to use
Dip Site Mark with BLUE — keeps your water source visible while you return for more.
Fire Line Mark with AMBER — track the fire's location relative to your position.
Dozer / Ground Crew Mark with RED — maintain situational awareness of ground assets.
Multiple POIs Use all three colours simultaneously to track different types of points on the same sortie.
Approach Watch the pointer as it moves toward 12 o'clock — you're heading directly toward your marked point.

Navigation Settings

  • Navigation is enabled by default — MARK buttons are visible on the flight page.
  • Toggle 🧭 Navigation in Setup to turn all MARK buttons and pointers on/off.
  • Waypoints are not saved between sessions — each flight starts fresh.
  • When Navigation is turned off, all active waypoints are cleared.

Tip: The pointers rotate around the gauge to show bearing relative to your current heading. The distance is always shown in nautical miles. Use the colours to quickly identify what type of POI each pointer represents.

Caution Panel Reference

The Caution Panel app replicates the physical Bell 214 caution/warning panel layout. Tap any light to view its procedure.

Bell 214B Caution/Warning Panel — tap any light to view its procedure

  • Use MAIN PAGE to return to the main app.
  • Select Bell 214B or Bell 214ST from the landing page to switch aircraft types.
