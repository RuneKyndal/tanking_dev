Installation

The app is a Progressive Web App (PWA). It must be installed to work offline — a browser bookmark alone will not cache the files. It WILL fail to load if offline.

iOS

  • Open the link in Safari (not Chrome or Firefox).
  • Tap Share → Add to Home Screen. Ensure the Web App toggle is on.
  • Close Safari and launch from your home screen to complete the download.
  • Grant location permission when prompted.

Android

  • Open the link in Chrome.
  • Tap the menu and choose Add to Home Screen → Install (not "shortcut").
  • Close Chrome and launch from your home screen to complete the download.
  • Grant location permission when prompted.

Poor connection at startup? If the app stalls on launch while on a weak signal, enable Airplane Mode to force it to load from the offline cache. Once loaded, disable Airplane Mode if you need GPS.

The app checks for updates automatically at startup. To force an immediate update, use Update App in the Setup page.

Quick Start

  • Select your aircraft type (Bell 214B, Bell 214 ST) — generic weights load automatically.
  • Adjust weights and fuel load to match your aircraft.
  • Tap GO START to move to the timer page.
  • Start the engine timer when you start engines. (optional)
  • Start the flight timer when you are ready. (optional)
  • Tap GO FLY — this ALSO starts the flight timer and opens the flight page.

Most inputs — aircraft type, gross weights, burn rate, ground speed target, and distance to fuel — are saved automatically and restored next time you open the app.

Fuel Endurance — Two Numbers Explained

The flight page displays two endurance figures simultaneously. They serve different purposes and are both required on screen.

Total Endurance

Fuel on board divided by burn rate — no reserve deducted. This is the number passed to flight services for SAR purposes.

Operational Endurance (+500 lb)

Usable endurance to the 500 lb reserve. This is the planning number — transit, tasking, and bingo calculations are all based on this figure.

Fuel Calc Toggle

The FUEL CALC button in the flight page top bar shows or hides the detailed planning section — Operational Endurance, Fuel Plan GS, Range, and Bingo-in countdown.

FUEL CALC green = planning section visible. Dark = collapsed, leaving fuel load slider, Total Endurance, and the always-visible Bingo-in summary. Useful when screen real estate is at a premium. Setting is saved.

Bingo Fuel

Bingo fuel is the quantity at which you must turn for fuel immediately — calculated so you can cover the Distance to Fuel at the selected ground speed and burn rate, and still land with 500 lb remaining.

  • Set Distance to Fuel to your routing distance to the fuel source before departure.
  • Bingo In counts down the time remaining until you hit bingo fuel. It is visible both inside the Fuel Calc section and in the compact summary bar inside the fuel box — so it is always on screen even with Fuel Calc collapsed.
  • The Range figure shows how far you can travel from this point before reaching bingo — useful for judging whether a diversion to a closer fuel source is viable.

MARK Buttons — Quick POI Marking

The flight page includes three MARK buttons positioned around the Ground Speed gauge. These are designed for quick marking of Points of Interest (POIs) during flight — water sources, fire locations, ground equipment, or any other location you need to reference.

How It Works

  • Single tap — marks the current GPS position as a waypoint and activates its pointer.
  • Double tap — clears that waypoint and hides its pointer.
  • Each MARK button has a distinct colour:
    • Blue — Water sources, dip sites
    • Red — Fires, hotspots
    • Amber — Dozers, ground crew, LZs
  • All three can be used simultaneously to track different types of POIs on the same sortie.

How the Arrows Work

When you mark a waypoint, a relative-bearing pointer appears around the Ground Speed gauge. Here's what it shows you:

Relative-bearing pointers around the gauge — bearing is relative to your heading, distance in nautical miles

Pointer Behaviour

  • The arrow rotates around the gauge to show bearing relative to your current heading.
  • 12 o'clock = directly ahead. 3 o'clock = 90° right. 9 o'clock = 90° left.
  • The distance to the marked point is shown in nautical miles next to each arrow.
  • If a pointer would go off-screen, it clamps to the edge of the screen so you always know which direction to look.
  • Each pointer has its own colour matching its MARK button.

Practical Use Cases

Navigation Settings

  • Navigation is enabled by default — MARK buttons are visible on the flight page.
  • Toggle 🧭 Navigation in Setup to turn all MARK buttons and pointers on/off.
  • Waypoints are not saved between sessions — each flight starts fresh.
  • When Navigation is turned off, all active waypoints are cleared.

Tip: The pointers rotate around the gauge to show bearing relative to your current heading. The distance is always shown in nautical miles. Use the colours to quickly identify what type of POI each pointer represents.

Ground Speed Gauge

The ground speed gauge is a deviation instrument — read it at a glance without focusing on numbers, the same way you read an airspeed indicator. Clockwise = faster; counter-clockwise = slower.

The target speed selector sits at 12 o'clock on the gauge — tap it to change the value. That number defines the centre of the arc. Min and max (±10 kts) are shown at 9 and 3 o'clock. Outside that window the needle pegs. The green band at 12 o'clock marks on-speed (±3 kts). The large central number is actual ground speed in knots.

  • Needle hard left — well below target speed.
  • Needle climbing toward 12 o'clock — below target, accelerating.
  • Needle in the green at 12 o'clock — on speed.
  • Needle right of centre — above target.
  • Needle hard right — pegged more than +10 kts above target.

Choose target speed based on conditions, pilot preference, and sea-scoop configuration.

Flight Log

  • After a flight, tap BACK on the flight page to return to the timer page.
  • Stop engine and flight timers.
  • Tap SAVE to record the entry — this stores date, registration, engine block time, and flight time.
  • Enter the aircraft registration if you haven't already.
  • Tap LOGS to view saved entries, or export them as .CSV or .TXT.

Log entries are stored locally on the device. Exporting to CSV or TXT is the only way to back them up — no cloud sync is performed.

Caution Panel Reference

The Caution Panel app replicates the physical Bell 214 caution/warning panel layout. Tap any light to view its procedure.

Access it from the ⚠ Caution Panel button in the Setup page.

Panel Test

Each caution/warning panel includes a TEST function:

  • Bell 214ST — Tap the TEST PNL area in the center of the panel. This toggles the displayed lamps on/off.
  • Bell 214B — Tap the round TEST push button at the lower right of the panel.

The test displays the lights that would normally be illuminated during pre-start checks.

Aircraft Selection

The landing page allows you to switch between Bell 214B and Bell 214ST panels. Each aircraft has its own unique caution light layout and procedures specific to that type.
